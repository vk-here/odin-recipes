Noodles

Description

Noodles are a type of food made from unleavened dough which is either rolled flat and cut, stretched, or extruded, into long strips or strings. Noodles are a staple food in many cultures and made into a variety of shapes.

The most common noodles are those derived from either Chinese cuisine or Italian cuisine. Chinese noodles are known by a variety of different names, while Italian noodles are known as pasta.

Cucumber raita generally tones down the spicy food. Since yogurt and cucumber (called kakdi or Kheera in Hindi) have cooling properties, they help to balance the heat from spicy food.

Ingredients

  • Noodles
  • Butter
  • Parm
  • parsley

Steps

  1. Heat 2 quarts of water with 1 tablespoon kosher salt in a large pot on high heat until boiling. Add the egg noodles. Cook for 5 minutes. Reserve 1/4 cup of the pasta cooking liquid and drain the noodles.
  2. Place the drained hot noodles in a large bowl. Add the butter and Parmesan and stir until the butter has melted.
  3. Stir in a tablespoon or two of the pasta cooking water. Stir in the chopped parsley.
  4. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Serve immediately.
